Interesting grammars:

A formula \(F\) is in \textit{conjunctive normal form} (CNF) if it is a
conjunction of clauses, where each clause \(C\) is a disjunction of literals:
\begin{align*}
  L &::= p \mid \neg p \\
  C &::= L \mid L \lor C \\
  F &::= C \mid C \land F
\end{align*}

Horn Formula
\begin{align*}
  P &::= \bot \mid \top \mid p \\
  A &::= P \mid P \land A \\
  C &::= A \to P \\
  H &::= C \mid C \land H
\end{align*}
